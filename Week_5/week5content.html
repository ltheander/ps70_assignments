<br> #### Week 5: 3D Printing <br> ![image of my final tree](./treefin.jpg) <br> <h3>Ideas and Getting Started with 3D Printing</h3> <p>During lab this week, one of the tasks was to 3D print a part that would take approximately ten minutes. I decided to go back to my week two project and 3D print the lego brick that I designed based off of a tutorial. I printed on a speed setting and scaled down the brick to half of its original size. Once I downloaded prusa and learned the 3D printing process, I printed the piece which only took five minutes. I was satisfied with the single brick, but then another student gave me the brilliant idea to print another one and see if they fit together. I then reprinted and upon putting the two pieces together, I found that they fit together perfectly.</p> <p>This gave me an idea for the project for this week; I would make real-sized legos of a variety of shapes that would all fit together to be assembled into a 3D structure.
